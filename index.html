The Opportunity — Robots are Coming

Deploy humanoid robots to handle repeatable, time-consuming tasks so clinical teams can focus on high‑value patient care.

The Problem

  • Nationwide ED staffing shortages (especially nurses)
  • Burnout from repetitive tasks
  • Skilled staff diverted from high‑value patient care
  • Rising complexity in emergency medicine requiring AI support
  • No formal curriculum to support the ecosystem

Dual Solution

  1. Create an ecosystem to train and deploy robots to assist ED Techs, RNs, and MDs
  2. Simultaneously build a curriculum to supply that ecosystem

Role-Based Support

Tech Support

  • Clean and prepare patient beds
  • Set up procedure kits (e.g., suture trays, central line kits)
  • Transport patients

Nurse Support

  • Deliver medications securely
  • Assist physicians
  • Provide status updates
  • Potential EMR integration to reduce data entry

Physician Support

  • Take patient histories
  • Generate differential diagnoses
  • Recommend appropriate tests

The Moat

  • Enterprise API license from Tesla
  • No training data leaves the company
  • Training protected by trade secrets and potential patents

Implementation Roadmap

  1. Partner with Scripps La Jolla Emergency Department
  2. Partner with Tesla for integration and licensing
  3. Offline training — master procedures before live deployment
  4. Deploy first robot for simple tasks (e.g., clean gurney)
  5. Expand tasks & integrate with EMR
  6. Add robots and scale to additional hospitals

Funding & Ask

  • Founder, Grants, Hospital Innovation Budgets, Angel Investors

Ask: Partnerships and early pilot site commitments.
